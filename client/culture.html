Ways to be considerate in 日本

Going to Japan is super exciting! It’s wonderful that you get the chance to go. Because this country is probably very different from your own, it’s important to understand that there are significant cultural differences that you will likely not know about when you arrive, so here are tips to keep in mind on how to avoid offending natives, to be respectful, and to stay safe.

• Don’t stick chopsticks in rice. -It’s reserved for funerals, otherwise it’s disrespectful.

• Slurp your soup! -It’s ideal for ramen, and suggests that you love it!

• Don’t pour your own drink.

• Accept gifts when given.

• Don’t tip. -Tipping is actually considered rather rude here.

• Feel free to drink alcohol in public. -Just know your own limits.

• Don’t blow your nose. -Most people there will simply sniff to avoid it.

• Try to be more open toward sexual openness. -The Japanese are more open about sex, for better and for worse.

• Don’t walk on the wrong side.

• Ask someone’s age. -They will ask you, because respecting elders is very important here.

• Don’t touch (if you can help it).

• Take your shoes off.

• Don’t walk while eating.

• Dress to impress.

• Don’t address someone by their given name (without permission).

• Feel free to call your waiter at a restaurant.

• Don’t get into the tub without taking a shower.

• Abide by “random” police searches.

• Don’t be loud on public transportation.

• Check if your medications are legal.

• Don’t maintain eye contact.

• Expect to be declined service sometimes.

• Don’t go to a tattoo parlor (unless they’re certified).

• Feel free to smoke in designated areas.

• Don’t be like Logan Paul, the annoying tourist. -That guy sucks and contributed to a poor outlook on foreigners.
